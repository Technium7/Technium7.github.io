Background

I am a Ph.D. researcher in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Cornell, working at the intersection of precision hardware, automation software, and optimization for complex experimental systems.

My work spans end-to-end system design: building cryogenic and ultra-high-vacuum apparatus, developing optical and electrical control stacks, and writing software that accelerates experiment design, calibration, and operation.

Selected Project: Laser Cooling

Rapid Multi-Mode Trapped-Ion Laser Cooling

Demonstrated phase-stable standing-wave laser cooling enabled by integrated photonics to improve motion control in trapped-ion systems. This project addresses a core scaling challenge in hardware performance and control reliability.

Built automation for daily calibration and in-situ analysis to increase test throughput, reduce operator overhead, and improve repeatability across long experiment cycles.

Project: Cryogenic UHV Apparatus

System Integration: Cryogenic, Optical, and Control Stack

Designed and built a cryogenic ultra-high-vacuum platform from the ground up, including multi-stage vacuum hardware, RF/DC filtering and stabilization, and UV-to-IR optical delivery for precision control.

This is a full-stack hardware/software integration project with direct relevance to robotics and controls: mechatronic system bring-up, sensing and calibration workflows, electrical/optical subsystem coordination, and robust operation under tight constraints.

Project: 6-Axis Robotic Packaging and Characterization

Automation for Packaging and Alignment

Built a table-top 6-axis robotic workflow for trap packaging and characterization on integrated photonics devices.

The system improves repeatability and positioning quality for alignment-heavy tasks and supports reliable, semi-automated operation in lab production workflows.
