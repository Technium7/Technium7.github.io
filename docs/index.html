Background

I am a Ph.D. researcher in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Cornell, working at the intersection of precision hardware, automation software, and optimization for complex experimental systems.

My work spans end-to-end system design: building cryogenic and ultra-high-vacuum apparatus, developing optical and electrical control stacks, and writing software that accelerates experiment design, calibration, and operation.

I am currently seeking robotics and control internships where I can apply this experience in automation, optimization, and integrated hardware-software systems.

Selected Project: Laser Cooling

Rapid Multi-Mode Trapped-Ion Laser Cooling

Demonstrated novel laser cooling schemes for trapped ions in phase-stable standing-wave fields enabled by integrated photonics. This work targets a key bottleneck in trapped-ion quantum computing systems and supports scalable, robust control of ion motion.

Alongside the experimental work, I built automation and control tooling for daily calibration and data analysis to improve repeatability and throughput.
