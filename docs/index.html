About me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Cornell, working at the intersection of hardware, software, and control to build trapped-ion quantum computers in Prof. Karan Mehta's group.

My work spans end-to-end experiment and system design: building cryogenic and ultra-high-vacuum apparatus, developing optical and electrical control stacks, and writing software that accelerates experiment design, calibration, and operation. I am now working on automating parts of the experiment preparation such as trap packaging and optical path maintenance with a robotic arm.

Selected Projects

Rapid Multi-Mode Trapped-Ion Laser Cooling

Demonstrated improvements in basic operations of trapped-ion systems using structured light fields enabled by integrated photonics. Reduced the ground-state cooling time by >10x to address the run-time bottleneck in state-of-the-art trapped-ion quantum computing systems and opened the door for scalable and efficient gate operations.

Building an AMO lab: Apparatus and Control systems

Designed and built a cryogenic, ultra-high-vacuum apparatus from the ground up, including multi-stage vacuum hardware, RF/DC filtering and stabilization, and UV-to-IR optical delivery.

Implemented mutli-stage control systems for laser frequency and phase stabilization. Automated daily calibration and in-situ data analysis to increase experiment throughput and reduce operator overhead.

Automation for Packaging and Optical Path Alignment

Built a table-top 6-axis robotic arm for packaging integrated photonics devices with fiber arrays. The robot arm helps improve repeatability and positioning quality for movement-sensitive tasks and supports reliable, semi-automated operation in lab production workflows.
